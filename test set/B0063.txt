    <p><font face="Arial" color="#0000FF"><strong>Internal <a name="Complaints">Complaints
    Procedure</a></strong></font></p>
    <p><small><font face="Arial">We hope that you are fully satisfied with the service you
    receive from the Society. However, if you are not, we have an internal complaints
    procedure (ICP) which is designed to help resolve your complaints in a fair and speedy
    manner.</font></small></p>
    <p><strong><small><font face="Arial">Making a complaint - ICP procedure</font></small></strong></p>
    <p><small><font face="Arial">You may make a complaint by any reasonable means, for
    example, by letter, telephone, e-mail or in person. All complaints should be directed to
    the Chief Executive and confirmed in writing.</font></small></p>
    <p><small><font face="Arial">We will send a written acknowledgement of your complaint
    within five working days (working days exclude weekends and bank holidays).</font></small></p>
    <p><small><font face="Arial">Within four weeks of receiving your complaint, the Society
    will either send you a final response or a holding response, which will explain:-</font></small><ul>
      <li><small><font face="Arial">why we are not yet in a position to resolve the complaint, and</font></small></li>
      <li><font face="Arial"><small>indicate when the Society will make further contact, which
        will be no later then eight weeks after the complaint was received.</small></font></li>
    </ul>
    <p><font face="Arial"><small>By the end of eight weeks after receiving your complaint, the
    Society will send you either:-</small></font></p>
    <p><font face="Arial"><small>a final response which</small></font><ul>
      <li><font face="Arial"><small>accepts the complaint and, where applicable, offers redress,</small></font></li>
      <li><font face="Arial"><small>offers redress without accepting the complaint, or</small></font></li>
      <li><font face="Arial"><small>rejects the complaint and gives reasons for doing so, and
        contains information about the right to complain to the Financial Ombudsman Service or,</small></font></li>
    </ul>
    <p><font face="Arial"><small>a response which</small></font><ul>
      <li><font face="Arial"><small>explains that the Society is still not in a position to make a
        final response, give reasons for the further delay and indicates when the Society expects
        to be able to provide a final response and</small></font></li>
      <li><font face="Arial"><small>informs the complainant that he/she may refer the complaint to
        the Financial Ombudsman Service if he/she is dissatisfied with the delay, and enclose a
        copy of the Financial Ombudsman Service explanatory leaflet relating to complaints.</small></font></li>
    </ul>
    <p><font face="Arial"><small>If you decide to pursue your complaint through the Financial
    Ombudsman Service you must do so within six months of the date of the final response.</small></font></p>
    <p><font face="Arial"><small>Further information relating to the current Financial
    Ombudsman Service complaints scheme is available from this Society or direct from
    Financial Ombudsman Service, South Quay Plaza, 183 Marsh Wall, London, E14 9SR, telephone
    number 0845 080 1800; e-mail: <a href="mailto:enquiries@financial-ombudsman.org.uk">enquiries@financial-ombudsman.org.uk</a>
    or website <a href="http://www.financial-ombudsman.org.uk" target="_blank">www.financial-ombudsman.org.uk</a>.
