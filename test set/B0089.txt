Decline decisions will be conveyed to the applicant/introducer at the earliest opportunity and the reasons for declining will be given in clear &amp; precise terms.							<li class="bodytext">The Society&#146;s mortgages can be repaid by either of the following two methods:							<ol class="bodytext" type="i">								<li class="bodytext">Capital and interest ( i.e. a repayment mortgage)								<li class="bodytext">Interest only (i.e. by which the interest is paid monthly calculated on the outstanding balance, but no capital is repaid until the end of the mortgage. The ultimate capital repayment is arranged via a suitable investment policy)							</ol>							<li class="bodytext">The maximum value of any advance will not exceed &#163;250,000 without Board approval.							<li class="bodytext">Minimum term for a mortgage is 5 years, with a maximum term of 25 years (pension linked mortgages may be extended to 35 years)							<li class="bodytext">All applicants must be UK residents.							<li class="bodytext">Maximum number of applicants per application will be 4.							<li class="bodytext">All applicants must be at least 18 years of age at the date of application.							<li class="bodytext">Upon completion of the mortgage term the youngest borrower must not exceed a maximum age of 70 years.							<li class="bodytext">At least one of the applicants must be in permanent full time employment or self-employment at the date of application. Normally, the Society will expect applicants to have been with their current employer for at least 6 months and have an uninterrupted employment history of 12 months.							<li class="bodytext">Self employed applicants will need to provide 3 years accounts.							<li class="bodytext">Fixed term contracts with 3 or more years to run will be considered as permanent full time employment.							<li class="bodytext">Contracts with under 3 years to run can only be considered if;							<ol class="bodytext" type="i">								<li class="bodytext">the current contract is for a minimum of 1 year with at least 6 months to run								<li class="bodytext">and the employer confirms a likely ongoing requirement for this type of work on similar contractual terms, or								<li class="bodytext">the contract has been renewed at least once (or have switched from &#145;normal&#146; employment to fixed term contract with the same employer)							</ol>							<li class="bodytext">Seasonal employment will not be considered							<li class="bodytext">Regular monthly payments must be made by Direct Debit to retain any customer schemes available.							<li class="bodytext">We will provide additional security for all mortgages over 75% loan to value, this premium will be paid by us where the loan to value is 90% or below. We will also cover this premium on 90-95% loan to value cases that are on the Lifestyle 95 product.							<li class="bodytext">Other additional security may be considered such as:							<ol class="bodytext" type="i">								<li class="bodytext">A charge on another property								<li class="bodytext">A charge on an investment account held with the Society (not TESSAs or ISAs)								<li class="bodytext">A charge on a life policy.							</ol>							<li class="bodytext">The raising of additional capital by a re-mortgage will generally be restricted to the following specific purposes, although other purposes may be accepted on their merits:							<li class="bodytext">Non-regulated purposes							<ol class="bodytext" type="i">								<li class="bodytext">Home improvements								<li class="bodytext">Purchase of freehold (not flats) or extension of leasehold								<li class="bodytext">Transfer of equity								<li class="bodytext">Purchase of land adjacent to the property							</ol>							<li class="bodytext">Regulated purposes							<ol class="bodytext" type="i">								<li class="bodytext">Purchase of a personal asset e.g. car or holiday home								<li class="bodytext">Consolidation of existing up to date personal debts e.g. credit cards								<li class="bodytext">Education fees								<li class="bodytext">Purchase of a home for a dependent relative							</ol>							<li class="bodytext">The re-mortgage option is subject to a maximum loan to value of 90% It is also not available on some of our products if any part of your existing mortgage is regulated by the Credit Consumer Act (i.e. a regulated loan) or if any part of your loan is for capital raising and the application is over 75% loan to value.							<li class="bodytext">All applicants will be subject to our mortgage and offer of advance conditions, as varied by these terms &amp; conditions. Please also refer to individual product terms &amp; conditions.							<li class="bodytext">In addition to the normal criteria the following restrictions will normally apply:							<li class="bodytext">We do not accept all types of flats for mortgage purposes. Please contact us prior to any application in order to obtain approval.							<li class="bodytext">Right To Buy Offer Notices must be in the same name as those person(s) applying for the mortgage.							<li class="bodytext">In all cases the valuer is required to confirm that a ready private market exists for the property to be purchased.							<li class="bodytext">The Housing Act 1985 outlines council pre-emption rights which allow for any discounts to be repaid on a sliding scale over 3 years (10 years in areas of outstanding natural beauty). Further advances sought during these periods will require council agreement to postpone these rights.							<p class="formtextbold">Unacceptable mortgage schemes:</p>							<li class="bodytext">Builders&#146; Gifted Deposit Schemes							<li class="bodytext">Shared Ownership schemes							<li class="bodytext">Properties with short leaseholds (less than 50 years)							<li class="bodytext">Concrete construction properties							<li class="bodytext">Steel framed housing systems							<li class="bodytext">100% timber constructions							<li class="bodytext">Prefabricated construction							<li class="bodytext">It is no longer the Society&#146;s policy to appoint a sole practitioner solicitor to its panel.						</ul>						<p class="bodytext">ALL LOANS SUBJECT TO STATUS.
