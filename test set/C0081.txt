                                    <p align="center"> 
                                    <font size="2" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif"> 
                                    <b> </b> </font> <font size="3" face="Arial"><br>
                                    <br>

 </td>
                                              <td background width="96%" height="4694"> 
                                                <font size="2" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">
                                                If you are a driver or owner of 
                                                a car, motorbike, lorry or any 
                                                other type of motor vehicle, you 
                                                must be insured by law. <br>
                                                <br>
                                                The world of motor insurance is 
                                                a complex one. There are literally 
                                                hundreds of different policies 
                                                to choose from, and it's not always 
                                                easy to know how to go about finding 
                                                the right one for you - and at 
                                                a cost that won't break the bank! 
                                                <br>
                                                <br>
                                                Here we aim to explain some of 
                                                the ins and outs of motor insurance, 
                                                and the advantages of obtaining 
                                                impartial and expert advice. Also, 
                                                what you should do if you're involved 
                                                in an accident.<br>
                                                <a name="#3"></a><br>
                                                <b><font color="#333399" size="2" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">THIRD 
                                                PARTY, FIRE AND THEFT</font></b><br>
                                                <br>
                                                The minimum insurance cover you 
                                                can buy is called 'third party 
                                                only'. This covers you if you 
                                                injure someone else or damage 
                                                their property. However, very 
                                                few companies will sell you this 
                                                cover on its own. <br>
                                                <br>
                                                The next step up includes 'fire 
                                                and theft', and this provides 
                                                protection against loss and damage 
                                                if your vehicle is burnt or stolen. 
                                                However it will not protect you 
                                                against damage to your vehicle 
                                                if you're involved in an accident, 
                                                or if it is vandalised.<br>
                                                <a name="#4"></a><br>
                                                <font color="#333399" size="2" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif"><b>COMPREHENSIVE 
                                                INSURANCE</b></font><br>
                                                <br>
                                                Comprehensive insurance covers 
                                                damages to your vehicle from a 
                                                wide range of causes, including 
                                                accident, third party, fire and 
                                                theft. Other benefits sometimes 
                                                include limited cover for items 
                                                stolen from your vehicle, medical 
                                                expenses and personal accident 
                                                protection. <br>
                                                <br>
                                                The word 'comprehensive' can be 
                                                misleading, however as these policies 
                                                always include limitations and 
                                                exclusions.<br>
                                                <a name="#1"></a><br>
                                                <font color="#333399" size="2" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif"><b>UNINSURED 
                                                LOSS RECOVERY</b></font><br>
                                                <br>
                                                This is a policy for motorists 
                                                which ensures that costs are recovered 
                                                from other parties in the event 
                                                of an accident which is not your 
                                                fault. It offers two levels of 
                                                cover and also provides Personal 
                                                Injury cover, roadside assistance 
                                                and a legal helpline. <br>
                                                <a name="#2"></a><br>
                                                <b><font color="#333399" size="2" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">MOTORIST'S 
                                                LEGAL PROTECTION</font></b><br>
                                                <br>
                                                This is cover to meet the cost 
                                                of defending court actions arising 
                                                from incidents on the road. It 
                                                includes a free 24 hour expert 
                                                advisory service.<br>
                                                <a name="#5"></a><br>
                                                <font color="#333399" size="2" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif"><b>HOW 
                                                MUCH WILL IT COST?</b></font><br>
                                                <br>
                                                The cost of motor insurance can 
                                                vary enormously - an insurance 
                                                broker who knows the market will 
                                                be able select the best policy 
                                                for you. However, many factors 
                                                will influence the premium you 
                                                have to pay.<br>
                                                <br>
                                                </font> 
                                                <ul>
                                                <li><font size="2" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">The 
                                                  type of vehicle you drive. Cars 
                                                  are grouped into 20 bands. The 
                                                  more powerful the engine, the 
                                                  more expensive the car is to 
                                                  repair, and the more likely 
                                                  it is to be broken into or stolen 
                                                  will all affect the band it 
                                                  comes into and the premium you 
                                                  have to pay. Generally speaking, 
                                                  insurance companies have the 
                                                  same bands, but some makes of 
                                                  car may rate higher or lower 
                                                  with different companies.<br>
                                                  <br>
                                                  </font></li>
                                                <li><font size="2" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif"> 
                                                  The area you live in, your occupation 
                                                  and where your car is parked. 
                                                  If you live in an inner city 
                                                  where you are more likely to 
                                                  be involved in an accident or 
                                                  have your car stolen, you will 
                                                  pay more than someone who lives 
                                                  in a country village. The same 
                                                  is true if you use your car 
                                                  for work. Whether or not your 
                                                  car is kept in a garage also 
                                                  makes a difference. Including 
                                                  cover for drivers under the 
                                                  age of 25 will also affect the 
                                                  premium - costs can be kept 
                                                  lower by restricting the drivers 
                                                  to the insured or the insured 
                                                  and spouse only.<br>
                                                  <br>
                                                  </font></li>
                                                <li><font size="2" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif"> 
                                                  Your age and driving experience. 
                                                  Young drivers are more likely 
                                                  to have accidents, while older 
                                                  people tend to be more careful. 
                                                  Insurers generally apply a basic 
                                                  rate to drivers aged 30-49. 
                                                  If you're younger than this, 
                                                  you will incur a 'penalty'. 
                                                  If you're older, you could qualify 
                                                  for a discount. Any disabilities 
                                                  you have will also be taken 
                                                  into account.<br>
                                                  <br>
                                                  </font></li>
                                                <li><font size="2" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif"> 
                                                  If you've been convicted of 
                                                  motoring offences in the past, 
                                                  or have a bad accident record, 
                                                  you will probably have to pay 
                                                  a higher premium.<br>
                                                  <br>
                                                  </font></li>
                                                <li><font size="2" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif"> 
                                                  Before an insurance company 
                                                  will sell you motor insurance 
                                                  it will want to weigh up all 
                                                  the risks involved, so before 
                                                  your broker can offer you an 
                                                  accurate quotation they will 
                                                  have to ask a lot of detailed 
                                                  questions covering the points 
                                                  outlined above. Remember - if 
                                                  you fail to provide accurate 
                                                  information a subsequent claim 
                                                  may be refused.<br>
                                                  <br>
                                                  </font></li>
                                                <li><font size="2" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif"> 
                                                  It's worth consulting a broker 
                                                  who has a wide knowledge of 
                                                  what's available and will shop 
                                                  around, saving you considerable 
                                                  time in having to seek quotations 
                                                  from different insurers.<br>
                                                  <a name="#6"></a><br>
                                                  </font></li>
                                                </ul>
                                                <font color="#333399" size="2" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif"><b>CUTTING 
                                                THE COST</b></font><br>
                                                <br>
                                                <font size="2" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">You 
                                                may be able to reduce the premium 
                                                you pay by restricting the cover 
                                                granted by the policy. For example 
                                                - you could limit the number of 
                                                people who will drive the vehicle. 
                                                But be careful - if the regular 
                                                driver is incapacitated, you don't 
                                                want to find out that nobody can 
                                                drive the car in an emergency.<br>
                                                <br>
                                                Another way of cutting your insurance 
                                                costs is to agree to pay the first 
                                                part of any claim. This is known 
                                                as an 'excess' and has the advantage 
                                                that you will know in advance 
                                                how much you will need to pay 
                                                towards any claim you make. The 
                                                policy may already be subject 
                                                to an excess, which you can generally 
                                                increase.</font><br>
                                                <font size="2" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif"><a name="#7"></a></font><br>
                                                <font color="#333399" size="2" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif"><b>NO 
                                                CLAIM BONUS</b></font><br>
                                                <br>
                                                <font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Once 
                                                you've had motor insurance for 
                                                a year or more without making 
                                                a claim, you should be eligible 
                                                for a 'no claim discount' or 'no 
                                                claim bonus.' This usually amounts 
                                                to a reduction of 30% on your 
                                                premium in the first year, rising 
                                                in annual steps to a maximum of 
                                                60 or 65%. <br>
                                                <br>
                                                If you make a claim, your no claim 
                                                discount will be reduced or disallowed. 
                                                If you have built up a higher 
                                                rate of discount, most insurers 
                                                will usually reduce it by two 
                                                years, rather than disallowing 
                                                it altogether, and some offer 
                                                a protected no claim discount. 
                                                Sometimes it may be cheaper to 
                                                pay for minor damage yourself, 
                                                rather than risk losing your bonus. 
                                                <br>
                                                <br>
                                                But what if the accident was your 
                                                fault? Your no claim discount 
                                                shouldn't be affected if the insurer 
                                                is able to recover the cost of 
                                                the claim from the party who was 
                                                to blame. Some companies operate 
                                                a 'knock for knock' or similar 
                                                type of arrangement. If two cars 
                                                involved in an accident are insured 
                                                with different companies, each 
                                                one pays for the damage to the 
                                                car it insured. This cuts down 
                                                on the paperwork and so the cost 
                                                of your premiums. However, this 
                                                practice is becoming less common. 
                                                <br>
                                                <br>
                                                It's important that you inform 
                                                your broker of all incidents of 
                                                damage or loss, however small, 
                                                and even if you do not make a 
                                                claim for them. There have been 
                                                cases where insurance companies 
                                                have refused to meet claims because 
                                                the driver failed to disclose 
                                                previous incidents.</font><br>
                                                <font size="2" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif"><a name="#8"></a></font><br>
                                                <font color="#333399" size="2" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif"><b>INSURING 
                                                YOUR CAR ABROAD</b></font><br>
                                                <br>
                                                <font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Your 
                                                UK policy will provide cover in 
                                                EU countries to meet their minimum 
                                                legal requirements, but this will 
                                                not be enough if you have an accident. 
                                                Nor will it cover you for theft 
                                                or damage to your car, and may 
                                                not cover your legal liabilities 
                                                to other people. If you pay an 
                                                additional premium you can ensure 
                                                full protection. Many insurers 
                                                will now give you free continental 
                                                cover for up to 30 days in any 
                                                one year, but you may need to 
                                                advise your insurer of any overseas 
                                                trip. <br>
                                                <br>
                                                <b>If you don't tell your insurer 
                                                and have an accident</b>, you 
                                                may find yourself paying for the 
                                                extra costs they would have been 
                                                incurred had the accident happened 
                                                in this country, provided the 
                                                insurer agrees to deal with the 
                                                claim. <br>
                                                <br>
                                                <b>Give your broker plenty of 
                                                notice of your trip</b>. He will 
                                                get the insurance company to issue 
                                                an international certificate of 
                                                motor insurance, otherwise known 
                                                as a 'Green Card'. This is not 
                                                absolutely necessary but is recommended. 
                                                <br>
                                                <br>
                                                <b>If you are driving in Spain, 
                                                you will also need a 'Bail Bond'</b>. 
                                                If you have an accident - even 
                                                if it is not your fault - the 
                                                Spanish authorities can impound 
                                                your car. A Bail Bond is used 
                                                to secure the release of your 
                                                vehicle. Most insurers will automatically 
                                                include a Bail Bond in your cover 
                                                up to a certain amount, usually 
                                                £1,000 if they know you're travelling 
                                                to Spain.<br>
                                                <br>
                                                <b>If you do not own the car you 
                                                are taking abroad</b>, it's advisable 
                                                to take a letter from the owner 
                                                explaining that you have permission 
                                                to drive the car. This should 
                                                save time if you have an accident. 
                                                It's also a good idea to take 
                                                the vehicle registration document 
                                                with you. It goes without saying 
                                                that you should never leave valuables 
                                                -including any documentation - 
                                                unattended in the car.</font><br>
                                                <font size="2" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif"><a name="#9"></a></font><br>
                                                <b><font color="#333399" size="2" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">IF 
                                                YOU ARE INVOLVED IN AN ACCIDENT</font></b><br>
                                                <br>
                                                <font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">If 
                                                you have a vehicle accident which 
                                                causes injury or damage, you must 
                                                give your name and address, and 
                                                details of your insurance policy 
                                                to anyone who reasonably needs 
                                                it.
                                                </font>
                                                <ul>
                                                <li><font size="2" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">If 
                                                  you're involved in an accident, 
                                                  do not admit liability. Take 
                                                  the name and address of the 
                                                  other driver, and details of 
                                                  their insurance company, and 
                                                  of any witnesses. If anyone 
                                                  is injured, call the police. 
                                                  If they do not attend the scene, 
                                                  or if someone else's property 
                                                  has been damaged, the accident 
                                                  must be reported to the police 
                                                  within 24 hours.<br>
                                                  <br>
                                                  </font> 
                                                <li><font size="2" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif"> 
                                                  You must tell your insurance 
                                                  company about the accident, 
                                                  even if you do not intend making 
                                                  a claim, as you will always 
                                                  need to submit a claim form 
                                                  to your insurer. Your broker 
                                                  can advise you about whether 
                                                  you should make a claim or not.<br>
                                                  <br>
                                                  </font> 
                                                <li><font size="2" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif"> 
                                                  If you intend to claim, you'll 
                                                  have to complete an claims form. 
                                                  You should wait for the go-ahead 
                                                  from your insurance company 
                                                  before having your vehicle repaired. 
                                                  They may want a damage assessor 
                                                  to look at it, or you may have 
                                                  to supply estimates before the 
                                                  work is done. Some insurers 
                                                  may have their own repair schemes, 
                                                  where you can take the vehicle 
                                                  to garages who may be able to 
                                                  start repairs immediately.<br>
                                                  <br>
                                                  </font> 
                                                <li><font size="2" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif"> 
                                                  If you have to pay a proportion 
                                                  of the repair costs, then you 
                                                  must pay this to the repairer 
                                                  irrespective of who was to blame 
                                                  for the accident. Should you 
                                                  decide to go ahead with the 
                                                  repair yourself, you are responsible 
                                                  for the cost until your insurers 
                                                  have agreed to pay. But beware 
                                                  of taking this course of action, 
                                                  because if the insurer can show 
                                                  repairs could have been done 
                                                  cheaper somewhere else, they 
                                                  may pay a lesser amount than 
                                                  you claim.<br>
                                                  <br>
                                                  </font> 
                                                <li><font size="2" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif"> 
                                                  If your vehicle is not worth 
                                                  repairing, the insurance company 
                                                  will offer you a lump sum for 
                                                  it. This is known as a 'write-off'. 
                                                  The damaged vehicle then belongs 
                                                  to them.<br>
                                                  <br>
                                                  </font> 
                                                <li><font size="2" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif"> 
                                                  After an accident, you may incur 
                                                  expenses which are not covered 
                                                  by your policy - examples are 
                                                  the cost of hiring a car while 
                                                  yours is repaired, or compensation 
                                                  for personal injury. Such expenses 
                                                  might be claimed against a third 
                                                  party - again, your broker will 
                                                  be able to give you all the 
                                                  advice you need.<br>
                                                  <a name="#10"></a><br>
                                                  </font> 
                                                </ul>
                                                <p><font size="2" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif"> 
                                                  <b><font color="#333399">IF 
                                                  YOUR VEHICLE IS STOLEN </font></b><br>
                                                  <br>
                                                  If you vehicle is stolen, you 
                                                  must inform the police immediately. 
                                                  You should also tell your insurance 
                                                  company and complete a report 
                                                  form as quickly as possible. 
                                                  <br>
                                                  <br>
                                                  You'll probably have to wait 
                                                  at least six weeks before your 
                                                  insurers will settle any claim 
                                                  for its loss. This time is to 
                                                  allow the police to investigate 
                                                  the crime, and hopefully recover 
                                                  the vehicle. <br>
                                                  <br>
                                                  If your vehicle is found, tell 
                                                  your insurance company and give 
                                                  them precise details of its 
                                                  whereabouts. <br>
                                                  <br>
                                                  Personal property stolen from 
                                                  a vehicle or damaged may be 
                                                  covered under a comprehensive 
                                                  policy, but only up to a certain 
                                                  limit - usually around £150.<br>
