<center><b>SKIPTON RESULTS HIGHLIGHT STRATEGY FOR SUCCESS</b></center>
<br><p>
Skipton Building Society is proud to report a strong all round performance for 2001 with the announcement of its Annual Results:
<br><p>
<center><b>HIGHLIGHTS 2001</b></center>
<ul>
	<li>Group Assets up 6.25% to £6.2 billion
	<li>Group Pre-Tax Profit, excluding exceptional items, up 11.22% to £53.2 million
	<li>Society Interest Rate Margin at 1.07%
	<li>Group Interest Rate Margin at 1.29%
	<li>Group Loans and Advances to customers up 5.89% to £4.7 billion
	<li>Group Retail Balances up 4.86% to £3.7 billion
	<li>Gross Capital Ratio up to 6.60%
	<li>Society Management Expenses down to 74p per £100 of Assets 
	<li>Skipton was voted a '5 Star' Mortgage Service Provider by readers of 'Financial Adviser', whilst Homeloan Management Ltd conducted the administration for two of the seven other winners of this award.
	<li>The addition of 4 new subsidiary companies, GMAP Ltd, Direct Life and Pension Services Ltd, Skipton Business Finance Ltd and Industrial Equipment Finance Ltd, saw Skipton increase to a 16 strong Group of companies, including the Society.
	<li>A unique 'Mortgage Discount Scheme' was launched.  This applies a discount (0.3% after 2 years rising to 0.5% after 4 years) to all qualifying Skipton borrowers whose interest rate is governed by the Society, either now or in the future, and whose current mortgage has been with the Society for at least 2 years.
	<li>Innovative new products were developed to create wider choice and value for members such as the Manhattan Mortgage (designed to track the 3-Month US $ LIBOR rate) and the Blue Chip Tracker and Guaranteed Protection Bonds.
</ul>
John Goodfellow, Chief Executive and Director of Skipton Building Society commented, "The year 2001 has, once again, proved to be one of considerable success, achievement and development for the Skipton Building Society Group.
<br><p>
"At the end of a difficult year, where we have seen increasing challenges both at home and internationally, we believe that Skipton is now uniquely placed to capitalise on the opportunities which our fast changing industry will present.  Our rapidly evolving Group structure ensures that we have a wide and varied range of interests across many sectors and these will, undoubtedly, reap benefits in the coming years.
<br><p>
"The addition of four new businesses to the Group during 2001 is further testament to our desire to explore appropriate opportunities to achieve a greater return on our capital. This will continue to be done in a prudent manner and in a way which does not inhibit the organic development of our primary business.
<br><p>
"Our corporate strategy, to remain an independent building society, is unambiguous. We are able to demonstrate that the more the Society and its subsidiaries prosper, the greater the long term benefits we are able to generate for our members in the form of better interest rates and services. It is imperative to remember that the one and only true measure of the benefits which apply to all members, is through an organisation's Interest Rate Margin. With Skipton's at 1.07% for 2001, we are confident that this, once again, will be amongst the very lowest in the whole industry.
<br><p>
"A fundamental part of our plan is that, unlike the banks, we do not have any external shareholders to satisfy, and so we can use this inherent advantage to focus on putting the interests of our customers at the very heart of our strategy.  There is already a weight of evidence which supports the fact that building societies offer the best long-term rewards to their customers, through consistency and fairness, when compared to the banks.  We believe that consumers will increasingly recognise that, whilst the interest rate on some new products may grab the headlines, the short term gains will be, more often than not, at the expense of long term advantages.
<br><p>
"We have continued to grow our business both organically and via acquisition and at each stage of our development, we continue to concentrate our thoughts on building a Society which is valued by our members, both present and future.  We believe that this coherent approach truly reflects the principles of a Modern Mutual in the 21st Century."
