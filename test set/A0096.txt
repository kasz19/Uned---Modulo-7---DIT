		  <p class="contentsubhead">Tailoring Your Home Loan</p>
		  <p>The key to Tailoring your Home Loan and it’s Repayment Schedule is <strong>choice</strong>, we explain the options and help you make an informed choice.</p>
      <p> We offer you <strong>flexibility</strong> and we give you <strong>contol</strong>. If you
        take control and Tailor your repayment schedule you will <strong>save money</strong>
        on interest costs and could <strong>save time</strong> by repaying your Home Loan
        early.</p>
      <p> You also have the <strong>peace of mind</strong> that comes from knowing that
        <strong>you</strong> are in <strong>control of your home loan</strong> and that we have the
        <strong>flexibility</strong> to cope with your ever changing needs. </p>

<p class="contentsubhead"><a name="link2"></a>Tailoring The Structure of Your Loan</p>

<p>There are a number of basic decisions to be made when you take out a Home Loan. </p>
<ul>
<li><a href="#borrow" class="link">How much to Borrow</a></li>
<li><a href="#term" class="link">The Term</a></li>
<li><a href="#interest" class="link">Capital and Interest Repayment or Interest Only</a></li>
<li><a href="#rate" class="link">Fixed, Variable or Capped Rates</a></li>
<li><a href="#repayments" class="link">Repayment Frequency</a></li>
</ul>
<p><a name="borrow"><strong>1. How much do you want to borrow</strong></a> - to a large extent this will be dictated by the property you want to buy. However you have the choice of how much of your own savings you wish to place as a deposit, which will influence the amount you need to borrow</p>
<p><a name="term"><strong>2. The Term</strong></a> - historically mortgages have been taken over a period of 25 years and there is often no choice in this matter. At Northern, we realise that you may have some personal goals eg. having the mortgage repaid before the children go off to university, or perhaps you want to retire early and have a specific age in mind, we therefore offer you a choice of the term over which your Home Loan is repaid, in order to accommodate these personal aspirations. We also realise that circumstances can change quite dramatically during the term of your Home Loan, so we give you the flexibility to adjust the actual term of your loan.</p>
<p><a name="interest"><strong>3. Capital and Interest Repayment or Interest Only</strong></a></p>
<p><a name="rate"><strong>4. Fixed, Variable or Capped Rates</strong></a></p>
<p><a name="repayments"><strong>5. Repayment Frequency</strong></a> - historically mortgages are repaid monthly, however Northern offers a choice of weekly, fortnightly or monthly repayments for our Home Loans, so that you can choose the frequency which best suits when you are paid. You can even choose the day of the week or the day of the month on which the repayment is made</p>
<p>At Northern we use the term "Home Loan" rather than "Mortgage". That’s because, technically speaking, a "Mortgage" is a legal document which secures a loan against property. What the Bank actually provides is a Loan which helps you to buy your Home, hence the term "Home Loan".</p>
<p>Making these initial choices about amount, term, type of loan, interest rates and repayment frequency, are the first steps in identifying what exactly you need from your Home Loan and making sure that it suits those needs. We call this process "Tailoring" and hence what we provide is a Tailored Financial Solution.</p>
<p align="right"><a href="#top" class="link">Back to Top</a></p>
<p class="contentsubhead"><a name="link3"></a>Tailoring Your Repayment Schedule </p>
<p>Once the basic structure of your Home Loan is determined we then work out your repayment schedule, we want to put you, the customer, in control - it is after all your Home Loan and Your Repayment Schedule.</p>
<p>With a Standard Northern Home Loan, your repayments go towards paying back interest only or a combination of both capital and interest. At today’s interest rates, your total interest payments can amount to a substantial sum. With a Northern Tailored Home Loan, you are encouraged to increase your Gross Repayments by an amount which you can comfortably afford.</p>
<p>There are a number of ways in which you can do this :</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="#monthly" class="link">Increase your monthly repayments</a></li>
<li><a href="#stepups" class="link">Annual Step-ups in repayments</a></li>
<li><a href="#adhoc" class="link">Ad-hoc Lump Sum repayments</a></li>
</ul>
<p><a name="monthly"><strong>1. Increase your monthly repayments</strong></a><br>
With this option you choose exactly how much you wish to repay each month, as long as it is greater than the minimum.  The amount you choose can be adjusted at any time, so you can pay extra when you can comfortably afford it and reduce your repayments at times of heavy expenditure such as Christmas and holidays.</p>
<p><a name="stepups"><strong>2. Annual Step-ups in repayments</strong></a><br>
With this option, your repayments remain the same throughout each year, however at the end of each year your repayments are "stepped up" by a percentage which you choose  eg. if you choose a 1% step up on your repayments of £500, your repayments will be increased by £5 (1%) at the end of year one and you will pay £505 each month the following year (year two) and so on.</p>
<p><a name="adhoc"><strong>3. Ad-hoc Lump Sum repayments</strong></a><br>
You may not want to pay an increased repayment amount every month and prefer to simply make extra lump sum repayments every now and again. If you receive a quarterly commission payment or perhaps an annual bonus, these can be used to pay off your home loan.</p>
<p align="right"><a href="#top" class="link">Back to Top</a></p>

<p class="contentsubhead"><a name="link5"></a>Bridging Finance</p>
<p>Bridging Finance is sometimes required to assist with the purchase of new property, either to provide the interim deposit payable when you exchange contracts or to provide additional funds between buying a new property and selling your existing one. It can also be used to make "stage payments" for properties under construction.</p>
<p>Your Mortgage Officer can assess your needs and where appropriate, arrange bridging finance. Security will be required.</p>
<p>The cost of this type of borrowing depends on the amount and duration of the loan and the interest rate charged. There may also be an arrangement fee based on the percentage of the amount advanced. These costs are therefore variable and should be considered independently after consultation with your local branch.</p>
