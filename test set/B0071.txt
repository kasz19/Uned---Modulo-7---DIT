						<p><font face="Arial,Helvetica,Geneva,Swiss,SunSans-Regular" color="#ff1493" size="2"><b>Complete Home Insurance at a Very Competitive Price</b></font><font face="Arial,Helvetica,Geneva,Swiss,SunSans-Regular" color="black" size="2"><br>
								
								
						When you're buying a new home, everything, it seems, needs doing at once. It is undoubtedly one of the busiest times of your life. It is also a time when just about everything needs working out very carefully indeed.<br>
								
								
						At Marsden Building Society, we can take at least one problem off your hands, that of insuring your new home and its contents.<br>
								
								
						In conjunction with Norwich Union Insurance, one of the UK's biggest insurance companies, we've developed one of the most straightforward home insurance policies. It's all based on the rebuilding costs of your home, a figure we have to assess before we can grant you a mortgage. We use this figure to calculate your full home insurance needs. You need not worry about contents insurance, that's included with a &pound;30,000 claims limit. Contents are even covered against accidental damage during the move to your new home (providing it is undertaken by professionals).<br>
								
								
						You need not worry about 'Index Linking', we automatically update your policy every year in December.<br>
								
								
						There is no need to worry about the premiums, costs are calculated on a very competitive basis, and you pay the premiums monthly together with your mortgage repayments.<br>
								<i>Everything really is just as easy as that and naturally, the cover you get is every bit as comprehensive as you would expect.<br>
								</i></font><font face="Arial,Helvetica,Geneva,Swiss,SunSans-Regular" color="#ff1493" size="2"><b><br>
									
									
						Valuables and Personal Possessions<br>
								</b></font><font face="Arial,Helvetica,Geneva,Swiss,SunSans-Regular" color="black" size="2">Generally speaking, the contents of your home are covered by the Homesafe policy up to their full <i>'new for old'</i> value, subject to a limit of &pound;30,000. These contents include items of furniture, furnishings, household appliances and equipment, carpets, clothing (excluding furs), linen and personal effects.<br>
								
								
						The same applies to most of your <i>'high risk</i>' items, defined as gold or other precious metals, jewellery, watches, furs, photographic equipment, pictures, works of art, binoculars, personal adornments, musical instruments, sports equipment and all tapes, disks and record discs associated with any of these items.<br>
								
								
						The one clause that we do insist on however, is that <i>'high risk' items cover is limited to &pound;1,000 on any single item</i>, with total cover for such items being &pound;7,500 within the home and &pound;2,500 away from the home. In addition, you have cover on your freezer and refrigerator contents, pedal cycles (up to &pound;250 per cycle), personal money (up to &pound;300) and external door locks (maximum &pound;500).<br>
								<b>The first &pound;50 of each claim is excluded.</b><br>
								<b><br>
								</b></font><font face="Arial,Helvetica,Geneva,Swiss,SunSans-Regular" color="#ff1493" size="2"><b>Eligibility for Homesafe<br>
								</b></font><font face="Arial,Helvetica,Geneva,Swiss,SunSans-Regular" color="black" size="2">Marsden Homesafe Insurance can be bought by borrowers whose home is of standard construction, provided it is not used for any business purpose or let and does not possess abnormally large amounts of 'high risk' items.<br>
							</font><font face="Arial,Helvetica,Geneva,Swiss,SunSans-Regular" color="#ff1493" size="2"><b><br>
									
									
						Homesafe Cover Means Safe Cover</b></font></p>

								<b>Accidental</b> breakage of fixed glass, sanitary fittings and underground services.<br>
								<b>Extended</b> accidental loss or damage to both buildings and contents may be included. This will cover such things as putting your foot through the ceiling, splashing paint on the wallpaper or carpet, breaking a costly ornament etc.<br>
