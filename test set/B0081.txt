<p><a name="a"><font color="#FF0000" size="3" face="Times New Roman"><b><i>How much should
I borrow?</i></b></font></a></p>

<blockquote>
  <p><a name="a"><font face="Times New Roman">To save yourself wasted time and energy it is
  wise to know how much you can borrow before you start house-hunting. </font></a></p>
  <p><a name="a"><font face="Times New Roman">How much someone will lend you and how much
  you can actually afford to borrow may be two different things. How much someone will lend
  you is usually related to your income. How much you can afford to borrow obviously depends
  upon your circumstances.</font></a></p>
</blockquote>

<p><a name="a"><font face="Times New Roman"><br>
</font></a><a name="b"><font color="#FF0000" size="2" face="Times New Roman"><b><i>How
much can I borrow ?</i></b></font></a></p>

<blockquote>
  <p><a name="b"><font face="Times New Roman">If you are a single applicant:</font></a></p>
  <blockquote>
    <p><a name="b"><font face="Times New Roman">Up to three and a quarter times your annual
    income.</font></a></p>
  </blockquote>
</blockquote>

<blockquote>
  <p><a name="b"><font face="Times New Roman">If you are joint applicants:</font></a></p>
</blockquote>

<table border="0" cellpadding="7" cellspacing="0" width="100%">
  <tr>
    <td valign="top" width="24%"><blockquote>
      <p><font color="#FF0000" size="2" face="Times New Roman"><a name="b"><b>either: I)</b></a></font></p>
    </blockquote>
    </td>
    <td valign="top" width="76%"><a name="b"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman">Up to three
    and a quarter times the main annual income plus one times the second annual income.</font></a></td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <td valign="top" width="24%"><blockquote>
      <p><a name="b"><font color="#FF0000" size="2" face="Times New Roman"><b>or ii) </b></font></a></p>
    </blockquote>
    </td>
    <td valign="top" width="76%"><a name="b"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman">Two and
    three quarter times the joint annual incomes.</font></a></td>
  </tr>
</table>

<blockquote>
  <p><a name="b"><font face="Times New Roman">If you are self employed we usually require
  three years accounts from which we generally assess the average of your net profit.<br>
  <br>
  These are the Society&#146;s guidelines and we are always pleased to discuss your
  individual requirements.</font></a></p>
</blockquote>

  <p><a name="c"><font face="Times New Roman">Remember you can give yourself more to put

</blockquote>

<p><a name="d"><font color="#FF0000" size="2" face="Times New Roman"><b><i>Does the value
of the property affect how much I can borrow?</i></b></font></a></p>

<blockquote>
  <p><a name="d"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman">The Society also needs to ensure that
  the property provides adequate security. For&nbsp; residential purchases we normally lend
  up to 95% of the purchase price or valuation, whichever is the lower.</font></a></p>
  <p><font size="2" face="Times New Roman"><a name="d">However, if the above is insufficient
  for your needs do discuss it with our Mortgage Advisors who may be able to help - it will
  cost you nothing to ask!</a></font></p>
</blockquote>

<p><a name="e"><font color="#FF0000" size="2" face="Times New Roman"><b><i>What is
Mortgage Indemnity?</i></b></font></a></p>

<blockquote>
  <p><a name="e"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman">If the amount you are borrowing is
  more that 75% of the purchase price or valuation, which ever is the lower, a Mortgage
  Indemnity premium will be charged. The one-off premium is usually added to the loan,
  although it may be paid up-front if you prefer.</font></a></p>
  <p><a name="e"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman">Part of this premium is used by the
  Society to obtain mortgage indemnity insurance, which acts as extra security for our sole
  benefit. This insurance will not protect you if your property is subsequently taken into
  possession and sold for less than the amount you owe.</font></a></p>
  <p><a name="e"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman">You will remain liable to pay all
  sums owing including arrears, interest and our legal fees. If a claim is paid to us under
  this insurance, the insurers generally have the right to recover this amount from you.</font></a></p>
  <p><font size="2" face="Times New Roman">In certain cases, the Society will pay the
  premium on your behalf for loans up to 85% of the purchase price or valuation, whichever
  is the lower. Please ask for details.</font></p>
  <p align="left"><font color="#FF0000" size="2" face="Times New Roman"><b><i>How do I apply
  for a Mortgage Loan<a name="e">?</a></i></b></font></p>
  <p align="left"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman">Once you have found your property
  and agreed a price you should contact your local Saffron Walden Herts &amp; Essex Building
  Society office to complete the application form.</font></p>
